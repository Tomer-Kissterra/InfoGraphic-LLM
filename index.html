Arm-Wrestling Strength Benchmarks

A look at the numbers behind Devon Larratt's training pillars.

Rising

  • Average: 20-23 kg (44-50 lbs)
  • Really Good: 28-32 kg (62-70 lbs)

Pushes your knuckles upward, a key leverage movement.

Pronation

  • Average: 28-32 kg (62-70 lbs)
  • Really Good: 38-42 kg (84-93 lbs)

Rotating the forearm outward to break the opponent’s wrist.

Back Pressure

  • Noob: 30-45 kg (66-99 lbs)
  • Pro: 45-55 kg (99-121 lbs)
  • Elite: 65-75 kg (143-165 lbs)

Pulling the opponent toward your body, a foundational strength.

Cupping

  • Primary Focus: High-Rep Volume
  • Devon Larratt's style: Up to 50 reps per set

Flexing the wrist inward to crush the opponent’s hand.
